My journey

How I got here

I grew up between two worlds. Born in Nepal, I moved to the United States as a kid and spent years making sense of that distance — between cultures, between cities, between versions of myself. By the time I reached high school in North Carolina, I had already lived in more places than most people my age and had learned to find stability in curiosity rather than geography.

I came to Northeastern because I wanted to build things that matter. The co-op model appealed to me — it meant working inside real organizations before I had the credentials to justify being there. At Harvard Law School I learned how large institutions think about infrastructure and trust. At Amazon I operated at a physical scale that reframed how I think about systems. At Fidelity, I get to bring that all together at the intersection of software and finance.

In between, I co-founded Apulza — a personal growth engine — because I kept noticing that the gap between who people want to be and who they are day-to-day is mostly a design problem. That felt worth solving.

Outside of work, I shoot photography, follow aviation closely, and spend time thinking about the kind of person I want to become. The movement across cities never really stopped — 11 cities, 2 countries, and counting.
